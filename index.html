cloud-independent load balancing as a service

    Deploy and test your code running on multiple free instances. No need to buyexpensive load balancers or computational power from PaaS.

How does it work?

Based on HAproxy, the load balancer enables you to bounce client request to an array of cloud instances hosted on different clouds

We take care of redirecting requests, cookies and all bandwith through our servers to one of the instances and by doing this we can ensure that we evenly share your inbound traffic amongst all your instances.

It's 100% transparent and 0% bullshit.

Use Case?

OK, so you are a startup and you want to design your API architecture. It should be distributed and scalable from day one. You want to use various free cloud instances and put them behind a load balancer without having to buy expensive services.

Setup Scenario

Buy your own domain, deploy your apps to any PaaS (Heroku, Apphb, OpenShift etc) and signup to apilb then:

$ curl -X POST --user "username:password" https://apilb.com/routes.json
{ "domain":"myproject.apilb.com", "routes": ["myproject.herokuapp.com","myproject.apphb.com"] }

SUCCESS!

Set up a CNAME record on your domain to point to the service domain:

myproject.com -> myproject.apilb.com

All the incoming traffic to your own domain will be transparently load balanced to your clound instances

Real Life Scenario
